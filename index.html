§02 · 5 AM every morning
Executive Chef · Morning Brief · Mon, Jul 7

Good morning. Here's today, and here's yesterday.

Today: Warm, high of 84°F, low chance of rain through close. No local events on the calendar, so expect a normal Monday — nothing to chase, nothing to brace for. One heads-up: your food cost will look heavy when you check tonight's numbers. That's just Monday prep. You know this — I'm noting it so it doesn't spook you at 9pm.

Yesterday (Sunday): Strong day. You did $1,240 in sales, up 12% over your last four Sundays. Your busiest window was 12–2pm — you were slammed by 12:15. Food cost landed at 28.4%, right on target. Labor ran a little heavy at 31.2%, about $22 more than your typical Sunday — closing took roughly 30 minutes longer than usual. Worth a look this week, not a fire drill.

Invoices: Your produce delivery came through clean. One item worth flagging — avocados up 18% this week ($1.20 → $1.42 each). That's the second week in a row it's climbed. Everything else moved less than a few cents either way, so I'm not bothering you with it.

Written Like An Owner.
Not An Accountant.

Every morning at 5 AM the AI Executive Chef reads your numbers — weather, yesterday's sales, food cost, labor, and what invoices moved overnight.

It doesn't bury you in every line-item change. Small price shifts get skipped. Anything that jumps meaningfully — a real cost increase, a trend three or four weeks running — gets flagged, with the number and what it's costing you.

It does not say "food cost was 31.2%." It says why — and gives you the trend so you know if it's a blip or a pattern.

Tuesday mornings add the weekly newsletter — the full week in numbers, a 7-day forecast, neighborhood intel, wholesale deals, and the full Recap Letter from The Auditor.

Day 1 it is good. Day 90 it knows your operation better than any manager you could hire.
